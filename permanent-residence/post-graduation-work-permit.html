What is a PGWP?

The Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) allows international students who have graduated from a designated learning institution (DLI) in Canada to gain valuable Canadian work experience.

PGWP Eligibility

To be eligible for a PGWP, you must:

  • Have completed a program of study at a designated learning institution (DLI)
  • Studied full-time for at least 8 months
  • Have a valid study permit when you apply
  • Apply within 180 days of receiving written confirmation of program completion
  • Have graduated from a PGWP-eligible program

PGWP Length

The length of your PGWP depends on the length of your study program:

  • Less than 8 months: Not eligible for PGWP
  • 8 months to less than 2 years: PGWP valid for the same length as your study program
  • 2 years or more: PGWP valid for 3 years

Note: Your PGWP cannot be valid longer than your passport expiry date.

How to Apply for a PGWP

  1. Obtain official confirmation of program completion from your school
  2. Gather required documents (passport, study permit, transcripts, proof of completion, etc.)
  3. Complete the online application through your IRCC account
  4. Pay the application fee ($255 CAD)
  5. Submit biometrics if required
  6. Wait for processing (typically 80-180 days depending on location)

Can I Work While Waiting for My PGWP?

Yes! If you applied for your PGWP before your study permit expired, you can work full-time while waiting for a decision. This is called "implied status."

PGWP to Permanent Residence

A PGWP is a pathway to Canadian permanent residence. With Canadian work experience gained on your PGWP, you may qualify for:

  • Canadian Experience Class (CEC) through Express Entry
  • Provincial Nominee Programs (PNP) in your province of work

Working in Canada on a PGWP allows you to earn Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) points for Express Entry and build the Canadian experience needed for PR pathways.

Common PGWP Mistakes to Avoid

  • Applying after the 180-day deadline — you won't be eligible
  • Studying at a non-DLI or non-PGWP-eligible program
  • Not maintaining full-time status during studies
  • Incomplete application or missing documents
