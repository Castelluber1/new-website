Post-Graduation Work Permit

What is

The Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) is an open work permit for international students who finish an eligible program at a Canadian Designated Learning Institution for up to three years depending on your program length.

How it works

Apply for the PGWP using your completion letter or final transcript. The work permit can be valid for up to three years, allowing you to work full-time for most employers and gain skilled Canadian experience that supports your permanent residency goals.

Who can qualify

Graduates who completed a program at a DLI, maintained valid status during their studies, and apply within the official timeline after receiving completion documents. Eligibility depends on your program, school, and study length.

Frequently asked questions

How long is the PGWP valid?

It depends on the length of your program. Eligible graduates can receive an open work permit for up to three years. We review your program and documents to estimate the likely validity.

When can I apply for the PGWP?

You apply after your school issues your completion letter or final transcript, within the allowed timeframe. We help you prepare everything so you can file as soon as you are eligible.

Can I work while waiting for a decision?

Some graduates may work full-time after applying if they meet specific conditions. We confirm your situation against the current rules before you start working.

Do I need a job offer for the PGWP?

No. The PGWP is an open work permit. You can work for most employers in Canada and build skilled experience that can support permanent residency.

Who is eligible for the PGWP?

Generally, graduates who completed an eligible program at a Designated Learning Institution, studied full-time for required terms, maintained valid status, and apply within the official window. Details can vary by program and school.

What affects the length of my PGWP?

The length is tied to your study program duration and what your documents show. Consistency across your letter, transcript, and forms is key. We double check all details before filing.

Can my spouse get an open work permit?

Spousal eligibility depends on your job and other criteria. We review your role and current policy to see if your spouse may qualify.

What if my study permit is expiring soon?

Timing matters. We map the safest sequence so you apply within the allowed window and protect your status while transitioning to the PGWP.

Do I need translations?

If any document is not in English or French, certified translations may be required. We advise exactly what IRCC expects for your file.

Why do PGWP applications get delayed or refused?

Common issues include missing documents, inconsistent dates or names, or filing outside the window. We prepare and cross-check your file to avoid preventable problems. Get your assessment.
