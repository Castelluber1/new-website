Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program (OINP): The Complete Guide to All 9 Streams in 2026

10 min read•2026-05-16

Ontario receives more permanent residents per year than any other Canadian province, and the Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program (OINP) is the single most powerful provincial lever in that flow. A successful nomination adds 600 CRS points if you are in the federal Express Entry pool, which functionally guarantees an Invitation to Apply at the next draw. For applicants who do not qualify federally, several OINP streams provide a direct path to permanent residence through the paper-based process.

This guide breaks down all nine OINP streams as they operate in 2026, the eligibility logic of each, how the Expression of Interest (EOI) system actually scores candidates, and the strategic decisions that determine whether your nomination application succeeds or stalls.

What OINP actually is, in one paragraph

OINP is Ontario's Provincial Nominee Program. It runs nine streams that allow Ontario to select skilled workers, recent graduates, entrepreneurs, and employer-sponsored candidates that the federal Express Entry system would otherwise miss. Eight of the nine streams use an Expression of Interest (EOI) model since 2021, where you register a profile, get scored automatically against stream-specific criteria, and wait for a Notification of Interest (NOI) at periodic draws. The Entrepreneur Stream is the exception and follows a separate business-immigration process.

Express Entry–aligned streams (the +600 CRS path)

These three streams require you to first have an active Express Entry profile in the federal pool. OINP draws from that pool when picking candidates, and a successful nomination earns you the +600 CRS boost that effectively guarantees an ITA at the next federal draw.

Human Capital Priorities (HCP)

The flagship stream. Targets Express Entry candidates with strong human capital (education, language, work experience) whose CRS scores are in roughly the 400-480 range, high enough to be in the pool but not high enough to clear general federal draws (which have typically cleared in the 524-550 range during 2024). Ontario periodically issues targeted NOIs to specific NOC codes (tech occupations, healthcare, education).

French-Speaking Skilled Worker

For Express Entry candidates with strong French (typically CLB 7+ French) and at least CLB 6 English. Cutoffs for French-aligned streams have been significantly lower than general HCP, reflecting Ontario's francophone immigration targets.

Skilled Trades

For trades workers in specific NOC TEER 2 and 3 codes who are already in the Express Entry pool. Requires 9 months of work experience in an eligible trade and a job offer or trade certification in Ontario.

Master's and PhD Graduate streams (no job offer needed)

If you completed an eligible graduate program at an Ontario university within the last 2 years, you do not need a job offer or even an active Express Entry profile to qualify for nomination. These two streams have historically been competitive because of their accessibility.

The Master's Graduate stream requires the program to be at least one academic year. The PhD Graduate stream requires at least two completed years of doctoral study at a publicly funded Ontario university.

Both streams use the EOI system. Scoring favors recent grads with strong language scores (often CLB 9+), Ontario work experience, and intention to settle in Ontario.

Employer Job Offer streams (job offer required)

These three streams require a permanent, full-time job offer from an Ontario employer at the wage threshold for the NOC. The job offer is the prerequisite, but candidates still compete inside the EOI pool for limited NOIs.

Foreign Worker Stream

For workers with job offers in NOC TEER 0/1/2/3 occupations. The offer must meet or exceed the median wage for that NOC in Ontario.

International Student Stream

For recent graduates of Ontario post-secondary institutions with job offers in TEER 0/1/2/3 occupations. Useful when the Master's or PhD stream doesn't apply (for example, college diploma graduates with a strong job offer).

In-Demand Skills Stream

The exception that targets specific TEER 4 and 5 occupations Ontario needs urgently, agricultural workers, food processing, long-haul truck drivers, construction labourers, nurse aides. The job offer must be in one of the explicitly listed NOCs.

Entrepreneur Stream (the non-EOI exception)

For business owners with significant net worth and investment intent (typically CAD $400,000+ investment, CAD $800,000+ personal net worth, 33%+ ownership). Two-step process: initial Expression of Interest, then a Business Performance Agreement after arrival. Direct PR nomination follows successful business performance.

This stream operates separately from the rest of the EOI infrastructure. Investment thresholds and timelines vary by business location (GTA vs outside GTA).

How the EOI scoring system actually works

Each of the eight non-Entrepreneur streams runs on a similar logic:

  1. Submit profile, you enter your data into OINP's online portal and the system auto-scores you against that stream's criteria
  2. Wait in the pool, your profile is valid for 12 months
  3. Periodic draws. Ontario issues Notifications of Interest (NOIs) to the highest-scoring profiles, sometimes targeting specific occupations or French speakers
  4. 14-day NOI response window, if you get an NOI, you have 14 calendar days to submit a full application with documents
  5. Decision, if your application is approved, Ontario issues a provincial nomination certificate

Common eligibility traps

"My CRS is high so I'll get an OINP NOI automatically." Federal CRS only matters for the Express Entry–aligned streams. For Master's/PhD/Employer Job Offer streams, it is irrelevant.

"I have a Toronto job offer so I qualify for the Foreign Worker stream." Not if the wage is below the median for your NOC in Ontario, or if your occupation isn't TEER 0/1/2/3. Confirm both before submitting.

"My profile is in the pool, so the clock is ticking on my immigration deadline." OINP profiles are valid for 12 months. If you don't get an NOI in that period, you can resubmit a fresh profile, but you start at the back of the queue.

"NOI = nomination." No. An NOI just gives you 14 days to apply. The application can still be refused if documents are incomplete or eligibility isn't proven.

End-to-end from EOI submission to PR card in hand: typically 12-24 months for Express Entry-aligned streams, 18-30 months for non-Express Entry streams.

Recent 2024-2026 OINP changes worth knowing

Ontario has continued to operate within IRCC's reduced national PR targets announced in the Levels Plan. This has translated to:

  • Tighter targeted draws (specific occupations only, generalist HCP draws have become rarer)
  • Continued strong emphasis on French-language candidates
  • Healthcare and STEM as the most common targeted NOC categories
  • Trades draws issued separately and less frequently

These trends fluctuate, so check the most recent draw history on the official OINP website before betting your strategy on a specific stream.

When OINP is the right move (and when it isn't)

OINP makes sense when:

  • Your federal CRS is in the 380-480 range, too low for general draws, but solid enough to compete in HCP
  • You have an eligible Ontario credential (Master's or PhD)
  • You have a qualifying Ontario job offer in a TEER 0/1/2/3 occupation
  • You are a French-speaking candidate (the French stream has the lowest cutoffs)
  • You have an in-demand TEER 4/5 occupation that matches the In-Demand Skills list

OINP probably isn't worth the effort when:

  • Your federal CRS is already 520+ (you'll get an ITA via general federal draw)
  • You have no ties to Ontario and don't intend to live there
  • Your occupation isn't in any of Ontario's current target lists
  • You're shopping PNPs without any province-specific advantage (BC PNP or Alberta AAIP might fit better)
