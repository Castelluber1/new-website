Best Province for Newcomers: Why 91% of Immigrants Choose to Stay in Quebec

6 min read • Feb 20, 2026

When choosing where to settle in Canada, immigrant retention rates tell an important story. Quebec maintains a remarkable 91% immigrant retention rate, significantly exceeding other provinces like Manitoba (44%) and Saskatchewan (39%). This guide explores why Quebec is one of the best provinces for newcomers.

How to Find the Best Province for Newcomers in Canada

Choosing the right province involves considering several factors: job opportunities, cost of living, community support, cultural fit, and quality of life. Retention rates provide valuable insight into how satisfied immigrants are with their settlement choice.

Key Statistics on the Best Province for Newcomers in Canada

Recent data shows significant variation in immigrant retention rates across provinces:

  • Quebec: 91% retention rate
  • Ontario: Approximately 85% retention rate
  • British Columbia: Approximately 82% retention rate
  • Manitoba: 44% retention rate
  • Saskatchewan: 39% retention rate

Why Quebec is the Best Province for Newcomers: Key Reasons

Cultural Vibrancy

Montreal and Quebec City feature diverse festivals, arts scenes, and culinary experiences. The province celebrates both French and English cultures, alongside thriving multicultural communities. From the Montreal International Jazz Festival to Just for Laughs, cultural events bring communities together year-round.

Strong Community Support

Quebec offers comprehensive immigrant support programs including language instruction (French and English), employment assistance, and community orientation services. Settlement agencies help newcomers navigate housing, healthcare, education, and social integration.

Economic Opportunities

The province features a diverse economy spanning technology, healthcare, education, manufacturing, and aerospace sectors. Montreal is a major tech hub with growing opportunities in AI, gaming, and software development. The lower cost of doing business compared to other major Canadian cities attracts employers and entrepreneurs.

Quality of Life

Quebec provides strong public healthcare, education systems, and social services. The province offers subsidized daycare ($8-9/day), making it easier for families to manage childcare costs. Public transportation in major cities is efficient and affordable.

Affordable Living

Housing and living expenses remain comparatively manageable versus other major provinces. Montreal, in particular, offers significantly lower housing costs than Toronto or Vancouver while maintaining excellent urban amenities.

What Quebec's High Retention Rate Means for Newcomers

A 91% retention rate indicates that the vast majority of immigrants who choose Quebec decide to stay long-term. This suggests:

  • Strong job markets and economic stability
  • Effective integration and settlement support
  • Welcoming communities and social networks
  • Quality of life that meets or exceeds expectations

Selecting the Best Province for Newcomers in Canada

While Quebec's statistics are impressive, the "best" province depends on your individual circumstances:

  • Language skills: Quebec emphasizes French language proficiency
  • Industry: Match your profession to provincial job markets
  • Climate preferences: Consider weather and seasonal changes
  • Family needs: Evaluate schools, healthcare, and community services
  • Cultural fit: Research the social and cultural environment

Next Steps: Moving to the Best Province for Newcomers in Canada

If you're considering Quebec or other provinces, research immigration pathways including:

  • Quebec Skilled Worker Program (QSWP)
  • Quebec Experience Program (PEQ)
  • Provincial Nominee Programs in other provinces
  • Express Entry federal programs
