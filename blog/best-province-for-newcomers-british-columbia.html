Best Province for Newcomers: Why 67% of Immigrants Stay in British Columbia

5 min read • Feb 20, 2026

British Columbia ranks among Canada's top provinces for newcomers, with a 67% retention rate among immigrants. Discover why BC's combination of economic opportunities, natural beauty, and multicultural communities makes it an ideal destination for your new Canadian life.

Understanding Retention Rates

Retention rate is a key indicator of immigrant satisfaction and success in a province. A 67% retention rate in BC means that two-thirds of immigrants who settle there choose to stay, suggesting they've found what they were looking for: economic stability, community, and quality of life.

Why Immigrants Choose BC

Economic Opportunities

British Columbia has a diverse and growing economy. Major sectors include technology (Vancouver's tech boom rivals major global hubs), healthcare, forestry, fishing, mining, and services. Vancouver is home to major tech companies, startups, and financial institutions, making it attractive for professionals and entrepreneurs.

The province also attracts skilled workers through programs like the BC Provincial Nominee Program (PNP), which offers expedited pathways to permanent residence for those filling labour market needs.

Natural Beauty

BC is renowned for its stunning natural landscapes. From the Rocky Mountains to pristine beaches and mild coastal climate, the province offers outdoor enthusiasts year-round activities including hiking, skiing, kayaking, and more. This natural beauty significantly contributes to quality of life and mental health.

Diverse Communities

Vancouver is consistently ranked as one of the world's most multicultural cities. You'll find established communities from Asia, Europe, Latin America, the Middle East, Africa, and beyond. This diversity makes it easier for newcomers to maintain cultural connections while building new networks.

Cultural Scene

BC boasts vibrant cultural offerings including museums, galleries, theaters, music festivals, and international events. Vancouver's film industry has earned it the nickname "Hollywood North," and the city hosts numerous annual festivals celebrating diverse cultures and arts.

Considerations Before Moving

While BC offers many advantages, there are factors to consider:

  • Cost of Living: Housing prices in Vancouver are among Canada's highest, which is the primary challenge for newcomers
  • Weather: The coastal region has rainy winters, though winters are milder than other provinces
  • Competitiveness: The job market, while robust, is competitive, especially in major cities

Is BC Right for You?

British Columbia is an excellent choice if you:

  • Work in technology, healthcare, or service industries
  • Value natural beauty and outdoor activities
  • Prefer multicultural, diverse communities
  • Can afford higher housing costs or plan to settle outside major cities
  • Appreciate temperate coastal climate
