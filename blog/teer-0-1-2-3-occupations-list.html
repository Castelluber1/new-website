TEER 0, 1, 2 and 3 Jobs in Canada: The Full Occupation List

Updated 2026 · Express Entry eligibility by occupation

For Canadian skilled-worker immigration (Express Entry), only occupations classified as TEER 0, 1, 2 or 3 count as "skilled work." If your job falls into one of these four categories, it can make you eligible. Here is what each level covers, with examples, and how to find your exact NOC code.

TEER 0

Management occupations

Jobs where you manage people, budgets or operations.

  • Restaurant, retail and store managers
  • Construction and mine managers
  • Financial, IT and engineering managers
  • Government and corporate managers
TEER 1

Usually require a university degree

  • Doctors, dentists, pharmacists, veterinarians
  • Engineers, architects, software engineers
  • Lawyers, accountants, financial analysts
  • Registered nurses, physiotherapists
TEER 2

College diploma or 2+ year apprenticeship, or supervisory roles

  • Electricians, plumbers, welders
  • Computer network and user-support technicians
  • Medical laboratory and dental technologists
  • Paralegals, firefighters, police officers
TEER 3

College diploma, short apprenticeship, or 6+ months on-the-job training

  • Bakers, butchers, chefs
  • Dental assistants, pharmacy technical assistants
  • Transport truck drivers, heavy-equipment operators
  • Hairstylists, estheticians

These are examples, not the complete database. Canada's official National Occupational Classification (NOC) has hundreds of occupations, each with a unique 5-digit code whose second digit is the TEER level. To confirm your exact code and TEER, use the official tool below.

How to find your exact NOC code and TEER

Search your job title in the Government of Canada's official NOC finder. The second digit of your 5-digit NOC code is your TEER level (for example, NOC 11202 has a second digit of 1, so it is TEER 1).
