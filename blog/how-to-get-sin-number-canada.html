How to Get a SIN Number in Canada: Step-by-Step Guide for New Immigrants

Published on May 26, 2026 • 8 min read

What is a SIN and Why You Need It

A Social Insurance Number (SIN) is a nine-digit identifier issued by Service Canada. It's the Canadian equivalent of a tax ID number and the backbone of your financial identity in Canada.

You need a SIN to:

  • Work legally in Canada (your employer requires it for payroll)
  • File income taxes with the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA)
  • Claim government benefits (Canada Pension Plan, Employment Insurance, Canada Child Benefit)
  • Open a bank account (most banks require it)
  • Get a mortgage or apply for credit

Without a SIN, you cannot work in Canada. If you're a temporary resident (study permit or work permit holder), you're entitled to apply immediately upon arrival.

Who Can Apply for a SIN

You can apply for a SIN if you are:

  • A Canadian citizen
  • A permanent resident (with PR card or confirmation of permanent residence)
  • A temporary resident with a valid work permit or study permit
  • A refugee claimant (while your claim is being processed)

You do not need a SIN if you are visiting Canada on a visitor visa (tourist) and not planning to work. However, you may want to get one if you plan to file taxes to claim refunds on tuition or medical expenses.

Documents You'll Need

Service Canada requires at least two pieces of identification: one primary document and one secondary.

Primary documents (one required):

  • Canadian passport
  • Permanent residence card (PR card)
  • Confirmation of Permanent Residence (COPR)
  • Canadian birth certificate
  • Passport from your country of origin

Secondary documents (one required to support your identity):

  • Driver's license
  • Credit card
  • Bank statement
  • Letter from your employer
  • School transcript or enrollment letter
  • Visa/permit letter from IRCC

All documents must be originals or certified copies. Photocopies are not accepted.

How to Apply: 3 Methods

Method 1: In-Person at Service Canada Office

This is the fastest method. You walk in, provide your documents, and receive your SIN on the spot (same day).

Steps:

  1. Find the nearest Service Canada office (call 1-866-SIN-2399 or search canada.ca/servicecanada)
  2. Bring your ID documents (primary + secondary)
  3. Arrive early to avoid lineups (mornings are quieter)
  4. Submit your application (takes about 10-15 minutes)
  5. Receive your SIN immediately

Wait time: 0 days (issued on the spot)

Method 2: Online via My Service Account

If you already have a My Service Account with Service Canada, you can apply online. This is convenient but slower.

Steps:

  1. Visit canada.ca/myserviceaccount
  2. Log in or create an account
  3. Find "Apply for a SIN"
  4. Upload scans of your ID documents
  5. Submit your application

Wait time: 3-5 business days (they'll mail you a letter with your SIN)

Method 3: By Mail

You can mail your application to Service Canada, but this is the slowest option.

Steps:

  1. Request Form SIN Application Form from Service Canada (phone 1-866-SIN-2399)
  2. Mail it with certified copies of your ID to the address provided
  3. Service Canada will process and mail you your SIN

Wait time: 10-15 business days

Cost and Timeline

Cost: FREE. There is no fee to apply for a SIN.

Timeline comparison:

Method Time Best For
In-person Same day Urgent (you start work next week)
Online 3-5 days Remote applicants
Mail 10-15 days Not urgent

Recommendation: Apply in-person if possible. You get your SIN immediately and can open a bank account the same day.

After You Get Your SIN

Once Service Canada issues your SIN, you'll receive either:

  • A physical card (if you applied in-person)
  • A letter with your SIN (if you applied online or by mail)

What to do next:

  1. Open a bank account: Most banks require your SIN. Bring your SIN card/letter and photo ID to a bank branch. You can open a chequing account in 15 minutes.
  2. Register with CRA: Create a My Account (CRA) login at canada.ca/myaccount-cra to track your taxes.
  3. Give it to your employer: Your SIN is needed for payroll and tax deductions.
  4. File your first tax return: Even if you only worked part of the year, file a tax return. You may get refunds or benefits you're entitled to.

Common Questions

Can I work before I have a SIN?

No. Your employer must have your SIN before your first payday. If you're hired, you can get your SIN in-person the same day you receive your job offer.

How long does a SIN card last?

Your SIN itself is permanent (you keep it for life), but SIN cards are phased out. Service Canada no longer issues new cards; they issue letters instead. If you already have a card, it remains valid.

What if I lose my SIN letter?

Call Service Canada at 1-866-SIN-2399 and request a duplicate letter. There's no fee. They can mail it or print it on the spot if you visit an office.

Can a visitor apply for a SIN?

If you're on a visitor visa and not working, you cannot apply. But if you get a job offer and switch to a work permit, you can apply immediately.

Do I need a SIN to open a bank account?

Most banks require one, but a few allow account opening with just a passport and proof of address. However, you'll need a SIN within a few months for tax purposes. We recommend getting your SIN first, then opening your account. Check our full guide on opening a bank account in Canada for more details.

Key Takeaways

  • Apply in-person at Service Canada for same-day issuance
  • Bring one primary ID and one secondary ID (both required)
  • There's no cost and no interview required
  • After getting your SIN, open a bank account and register with CRA
  • Keep your SIN letter in a safe place (you'll need it for taxes and benefits)
