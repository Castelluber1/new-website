What is an Open Work Permit (OWP)

A temporary permit that authorizes legal work in Canada without needing a job offer. It’s common for spouses of students or workers, and also for recent graduates from eligible institutions. With this permit, you can work for almost any employer in the country.

Types of Open Work Permits

See below the main options to qualify for an open work permit in Canada.

Spouse of a Student

If your spouse is studying at an eligible Canadian institution full-time, you may be eligible for an OWP and work full-time.

Spouse of a Worker

If your spouse holds a qualifying job in Canada, you may be eligible for an open work permit.

PGWP

Allows graduates from Designated Learning Institutions (DLIs) to work in Canada after completing their program, for up to 3 years depending on program length.

Bridging OWP

For applicants who have already applied for permanent residence and wish to keep working legally while waiting for a decision.

Vulnerable Worker OWP

For temporary workers facing abuse in the workplace who need to leave their current employer to protect themselves.

IEC Working Holiday

Open work permit for youth from countries with agreements with Canada, usually through a lottery or annual quotas.

Frequently asked questions

Can I work in any field with an OWP?

Yes, except for specific restrictions printed on the document.

Can I study with an OWP?

Short courses are generally allowed; for regular academic studies, you need a study permit.

Does my student spouse need to study full-time?

Yes, and in an eligible program.

Can I renew a PGWP?

Normally no; only under specific public policies.

Can I apply from inside Canada?

It depends on your category and current status.

How long does it take?

It varies; we align expectations during the consultation.

Can I travel while I wait?

Check your entry status and TRV/eTA as applicable.

What can cause a refusal?

Insufficient relationship proof, invalid status, incorrect category, or inconsistent information.
